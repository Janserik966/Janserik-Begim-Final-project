Nature

Kolsai Lakes (Kazakh: Kөлсай көлдері) are a system of three lakes located in the northern Tien Shan Mountains, on the Kolsai River, in the natural corridor connecting the Kungey Alatau and Zailiysky Alatau mountain ranges. The lakes are situated in the Kegen District of the Almaty Region of Kazakhstan, about 10 km north of the border with Kyrgyzstan and 330 km southeast of Almaty. They lie at elevations of approximately 1000 m, 2500 m, and 2700 m above sea level. The lakes are often called the “Pearl of the Northern Tien Shan.” They are surrounded by coniferous forests of Tien Shan spruce. The depth of the lakes reaches up to 50 meters. Among the wildlife found here is rainbow trout. The Kolsai Lakes consist of three lakes: The Third Lake — located at an altitude of 1818 m; its length is 1 km, width 400 m, and depth 39 m. The Middle (Second) Kolsai Lake (Mynzhylgy) — located at an altitude of 2252 m; it is the largest of the three lakes. The First Kolsai Lake (Upper Kolsai) — located at an altitude of 2850 m.

Astana

Astana (Astana) is the capital of Kazakhstan. It is located in the north-central part of the country, on the Ishim River. The city was founded in 1862 as Akmola and was renamed Astana in 1997. Astana is a modern city with a population of over 1 million. It is home to the government of Kazakhstan, as well as a number of universities, museums, and theaters. Astana is a popular tourist destination and is known for its many architectural monuments, including the Baiterek Tower and the Khan Shatyr entertainment center. In 2019, its name was changed from Astana to Nur-Sultan in honor of the country's first president, Nursultan Nazarbayev. It is now known as Astana again.
